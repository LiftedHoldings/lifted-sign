02 The service

Lifted Sign is a tool for preparing, sending, and executing documents electronically. It lets you upload documents, place signature and data fields, route them to one or more signers, capture electronic signatures, and receive a tamper-evident, AES-256 / PAdES-sealed final document together with a Certificate of Completion that records the audit trail.

Signers you designate receive a unique, single-purpose link by email to review and sign the document you send them; they do not need their own account to sign. An electronic signature captured through Lifted Sign is intended to be legally binding under the U.S. federal ESIGN Act and the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) when the required consent and intent are captured (see Section 7).

You are responsible for your documents. Lifted Sign is a signing tool, not a law firm, and is not a party to the agreements you execute through it. Whether a given document is valid, enforceable, and appropriate for electronic signature in your jurisdiction and for your transaction is your responsibility. Some records — for example certain wills, testamentary trusts, court filings, notices of default or foreclosure, and instruments requiring notarization — may be excluded from electronic signature under applicable law. 07 Electronic records & signatures

This section is your consent to do business electronically through Lifted Sign, as contemplated by the U.S. federal Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (ESIGN, 15 U.S.C. § 7001 et seq.) and the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act (UETA) as adopted in most U.S. states.

Consent to transact electronically

Before signing, each signer is presented with a consent disclosure and asked to agree to use electronic records and signatures for the transaction. That consent, together with the signer's affirmative act of signing (their intent to sign), is captured and bound into the record along with the signer's name, email, IP address, and event timestamps.

Legal effect

When completed this way, an electronic signature and record have the same legal effect as a handwritten signature and a paper record, and will not be denied legal effect solely because they are electronic. You agree that a completed document, its Certificate of Completion, and the associated audit trail may be used as evidence of the agreement and of each signer's assent.

Right to paper & to withdraw consent

Consent to sign electronically is voluntary. Before completing a signature, a signer may decline to proceed electronically and, where applicable, request a paper or non-electronic copy of the record from the sender. A signer may also withdraw consent to future electronic transactions by declining to sign or by contacting the sending party. Withdrawing consent, or requesting paper, does not affect the legal validity of documents already completed and signed electronically. Because the sender — not the operator — controls the underlying transaction, requests for paper copies or to withdraw from a specific transaction should be directed to the party who sent the document.

Hardware & software you need

To access, sign, and retain electronic records through Lifted Sign, you need: a device with internet access; a current, standards-compliant web browser; a valid email account able to receive messages from the sender and the Service; the ability to view, download, and print or save PDF files; and enough storage or a printer to retain copies of the records for your files. If these requirements change in a way that affects your ability to access records, the change will be reflected here. By continuing to use the Service you confirm you can access records in these formats.
