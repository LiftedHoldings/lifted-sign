02 What we collect

We collect only what we need to run the signing service. That falls into a few clear buckets:

Account information

  • Your email and name, so we can create your account, address your documents, and reach you about documents you send or receive.
  • Authentication data — depending on how this install is configured you may sign in with Google, an email sign-in link, an email and password, or a phone number and one-time code. If you enable it, we store a phone number for SMS two-factor codes and/or an authenticator-app (TOTP) secret. Passwords, when used, are stored only as salted hashes — never in plain text.

Documents you upload

  • The PDFs and files you upload to send for signature, the fields you place on them, and the finished, executed documents — along with whatever personal information you choose to put inside them.

Signer records (audit trail)

To make an electronic signature enforceable, each document carries an audit trail. For every signer we record:

  • Name and email address of the signer, and the unique link they used to access the document.
  • IP address and timestamps for key events — when the document was sent, opened, consented to, and signed.
  • ESIGN / UETA consent records — proof that each signer agreed to sign electronically before the first field was completed.

Usage & technical data

  • Log and device data — such as IP address, browser/user-agent, request timestamps, and error and security events — generated automatically when you use the Service, used to keep it running, secure, and abuse-free.
  • Essential cookies — a session cookie to keep you signed in and protect your session (see Cookies & sessions).

We do not collect payment card details during beta. Lifted Sign is free with no card required and no payment flow anywhere in the product. If paid plans are introduced later, any billing information would be handled by a dedicated payment processor — never stored on our servers in full.

03 How we use it

We use the information above strictly to deliver and protect the signing service:

  • Deliver the service — build and route your documents, present the signing experience to each party, and return the finished document.
  • Maintain the legal audit trail — capture and preserve consent, identity, IP, and timestamp records so a completed signature is enforceable and its history verifiable.
  • Seal and certify — freeze each completed document, apply an AES-256 / PAdES seal, and generate a Certificate of Completion with a SHA-256 fingerprint and the full audit trail.
  • Security and integrity — verify identity at sign-in, apply rate limits, detect and prevent abuse, and keep a tamper-evident record of what was signed and by whom.
  • Communicate with you — send signing requests, completion notices, and essential account or service messages. These are transactional; we do not send marketing email without your consent.

We do not sell or rent your personal information, and we do not use your documents to serve advertising or to train advertising or unrelated machine-learning models.

06 Data retention

We keep your account information and documents for as long as your account is active, so your signed records stay available to you. Executed documents, their audit trails, and Certificates of Completion are retained as part of the durable, tamper-evident record of an agreement — that permanence is a feature of a signing service, and a signer or sender may need to prove what was agreed years later.

When you delete a document or close your account, we remove the associated data from our active systems. Residual copies may persist briefly in encrypted backups before they age out on our normal backup cycle. We may retain limited records where the law requires it, or where they are needed to preserve the integrity of a completed agreement's audit trail or to resolve a dispute.

07 Security

Protecting signed records is the core of what we do.

  • Encryption in transit — all traffic to Lifted Sign is served over TLS.
  • Encryption at rest — documents and signatures are encrypted in storage.
  • Tamper-evident seal — when the last field is signed, the document is frozen, AES-256 / PAdES sealed, and fingerprinted with a SHA-256 hash recorded on the Certificate of Completion. Alter one byte and the fingerprint no longer matches — proof the record is exactly what everyone agreed to.
  • Access controls — sign-in via Google or email and password, with optional SMS two-factor authentication.

No online service can promise perfect security, and we don't. What we promise is honest engineering: encryption in transit and at rest, a tamper-evident seal, and no fabricated certifications we don't hold.
